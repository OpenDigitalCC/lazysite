Release 0.5.28 - Manager bar no longer overlaps themed headers

Full suite green (1993 tests); perlcritic clean; strict SBOM gate green.  Channel: edge.

The black manager bar was pinned to the top of the viewport (position:fixed), so on a
theme with its own sticky or fixed header - like the explorer theme - the two sat on
top of each other and the header slid under the bar when you scrolled. There is no way
for the engine to reach into an arbitrary theme and offset its header, so the bar now
sits in the normal page flow at the very top and scrolls away with the page. It no
longer overlaps any theme, and themes need to do nothing.

Trade-off: the bar is no longer always on screen - scroll back to the top to reach
Edit / Manage / Sign out. (If you would rather it stayed pinned, the alternative is a
small floating corner launcher; say the word and we can switch to that.)

The bar remains a manager-only feature: anonymous visitors and logged-in members
without Manager-UI access do not see it - now locked in by a regression test for the
member case.
