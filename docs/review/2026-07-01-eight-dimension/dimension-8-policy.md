## Findings

### F8.1 - Posture declaration present but carries two factual errors (WARN)

`docs/POLICY.md` exists, declares the Commercial regime, and explicitly does not claim conformity it lacks ("This document is the posture of record; it does not claim conformity that is not yet in place") - the honest-declaration shape the framework wants. Two defects:

- `docs/POLICY.md:11` cites the "Cyber Resilience Act (Reg. 2024/1689)". That is the AI Act's number. The CRA is **Regulation (EU) 2024/2847** (framework POLICY.md:145,186-189). In the compliance document of record, a wrong legal citation would propagate into the Declaration of Conformity verbatim. Classification: WARN now; it becomes REFUSE the moment a DoC is drafted from this text.
- `docs/POLICY.md:11` and `:33` say "seven-dimension framework" / "(7-dimension)". The framework is eight-dimension; the document predates the split (a `docs/review/2026-06-23-seven-dimension-review.md` exists). Currency defect in the posture of record.

### F8.5 - Regime fit: Commercial is the right declaration (PASS)

`docs/POLICY.md` selects Commercial and explicitly declines the commercial-regulated overlay. Sanity check against what lazysite is: an operator-deployed CGI site builder/CMS, offered commercially and used in client deliveries (IMPLEMENTATION.md:141), handling credentials, TOTP seeds and form submissions, exposed to external AI publishing partners. It is not safety-relevant, not sector-regulated, not an AI system, and not an ISO 27001-scoped managed service in its own right - so the CRA-plus-OpenChain Commercial posture is correct, and the declaration matches IMPLEMENTATION.md's own classification of lazysite as commercial. Two adjacent notes for the record:

- IMPLEMENTATION.md:871 proposes relicensing lazysite to AGPL-3.0-only (proposed, unconfirmed) while `LICENSE` and POLICY.md say MIT. POLICY.md correctly reflects the current reality; when the licensing decision lands it must be reflected here and in the SBOM's licence fields.
- Where the team hosts lazysite for clients, IMPLEMENTATION.md:793 attaches an operator role and CSF posture to that hosting - a separate posture, correctly not folded into this product-level POLICY.md.
